For over 90 years the orangutan population has been swiftly decreasing due to human effects. Oil palm plantations have spread throughout the world to countries like Indonesia, Malaysia, and parts of Thailand as shown on the next graph below. The approximate population has dropped by a shocking 58.7% over only the 90 years of data shown.

As the orangutan population is decaying, the amount of oil palm plantations has skyrocketed from 3.26 million hectares worldwide in 1970 to over 27.26 million in 2020
